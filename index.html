You may register for the Bachelor Thesis once you have completed at least 172 ECTS (148 ECTS for earlier intakes) and provided proof of a completed 6-week Basic Internship.

DAAD students doing the thesis in Germany register and submit through the RUB office - contact Dr. Monika Thol (Academic Coordinator, RUB).

Q & A

Can I split the 14 weeks across two companies?
Yes, one or two companies is fine. Each company must sign and stamp to confirm the duration and content you did there.

If the first company covers all 4 fields, must I write about a second?
If you commit to 4 specific fields for 14 weeks, spend roughly equal time per field (about 4 weeks each). Otherwise, write up every field and content you actually did, certified and stamped by the companies.

The attestation lists every week. Can I group weeks with the same work?
Yes, you can group them.

Can I structure the report into 4 FP-area sections instead of week-by-week?
Yes. Just make sure you cover at least 4 FPs.

How long should each FP section be?
It depends on your work; there is no fixed page count.

Is there a sample report from a previous student?
Yes, see "Internship Report_Sample" in the guidelines folder.

My contract is about 12 weeks. Is that enough?
You must prove a full 14 weeks. Ask your company to extend by two more weeks if possible.

Where are the attestation form and report format?
In the guidelines folder (see "Guidelines, Templates & Sample").

Do I need a signature and stamp for each FP, and for the attestation?
Yes. Each FP needs the student's signature plus the company's signature and stamp; the same applies to the attestation.

What is the grade scale?
No grades, Passed / Failed only.

Will my FPP (interdisciplinary project) count, and is overlap with another student OK?
First give the MEN coordinator your project content so they can assess it. You must cover at least 4 of the 17 FPs. Who you work with does not matter; what matters is what you did and wrote.

Can my company sign digitally?
Yes, digital signatures and PDF reports are accepted. You must still submit a hard-copy report later.

For a 6-month internship, must I detail the whole period?
Focus on the tasks completed during the 14 weeks as outlined in the guidelines.

04

Exam & Schedule

Attendance, exam attempts, step-back rules, and the semester schedules.

Attendance

≥ 75% to sit the exam

Attempts

Max 4 per course

Step back

From attempt 2, max 2×

Step-back deadline

≥ 2 weeks before exam

You must attend at least 75% of class hours to be eligible for the final exam; otherwise you are barred and receive a Fail. Each course allows a maximum of four exam attempts. You may re-sit without re-taking the course on attempts 1, 2 and 4; failing the same course twice requires re-taking it before the 3rd attempt.

Step back (exam postponement) is available from the 2nd attempt onward, up to two times per course, and must be requested at least two weeks before the exam date. Registering for a course counts as registering for its exam.

Graduation

Requirements, grade classification and the graduation ceremony.

Requirements

To graduate you must: pass German 1, 2, 3 and 4; pass the Basic Internship, Professional Internship and Bachelor Thesis; submit the internship and thesis hard-copy reports; and complete 210 ECTS (180 ECTS for earlier intakes).

RUB Classification

Grade: very good ≥ 0.7 and ≤ 1.5; good > 1.5 and ≤ 2.5; satisfactory > 2.5 and ≤ 3.5; sufficient > 3.5 and ≤ 4.0.

Overall grade: with distinction - overall grade ≥ 0.7 and < 1.3 and Bachelor’s Thesis grade ≥ 0.7 and ≤ 1.3; very good ≤ 1.5; good > 1.5 and ≤ 2.5; satisfactory > 2.5 and ≤ 3.5; sufficient > 3.5 and ≤ 4.0.

ECTS = Credit Points

Ceremony & Certificates

The graduation ceremony is held once a year in November. Certificates can be collected in November or April; to be ready, the certificate must be issued about two months in advance. For certificate and transcript authorization, bring your original documents to the People’s Committee of your nearest ward.
